# Code of conduct

This file isn't a contract — it's a friendly set of expectations for
anyone participating in the OpenFOV project. The point is just to keep
the project a place where people enjoy contributing.

## What we're going for

- **Be kind.** People are at different skill levels, on different
  hardware, in different time zones, and dealing with different stuff
  in their lives. Default to charity when someone asks a question or
  files a bug.
- **Be constructive.** "This doesn't work" is less useful than "this
  doesn't work — here's what I tried." Critique ideas, not people.
- **Be patient.** This is a side project. Maintainers and contributors
  respond when they can.
- **Be specific.** "It's broken" is hard to act on. Logs, screenshots,
  versions, and reproducible steps make everyone's life easier.

## What's not OK

- Personal attacks, name-calling, harassment, or targeted abuse —
  whether public or in DMs related to the project.
- Discrimination or slurs of any kind.
- Sustained disruption of discussions or repository activity.
- Posting other people's private information without permission.

## If something goes wrong

If you see behavior that's making the project worse, you can:

- Reply in the thread and steer it back to constructive territory.
- Or email the maintainer at **epalosh@icloud.com** with the link and
  a short summary.

There's no formal review board. The maintainer reads reports, applies
common sense, and may edit comments, lock threads, or block accounts
when something obviously needs it. This is judgment-based, not
legal-grade — appeals are also just an email.

## Scope

This applies to interactions in this repo (issues, PRs, discussions,
comments) and anywhere else where you're representing OpenFOV.

## Acknowledgments

The spirit here borrows from the [Contributor Covenant](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/)
without copying its formal language verbatim. We're going for "be a
reasonable adult" rather than legalese.
